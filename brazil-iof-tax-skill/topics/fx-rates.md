## Art. 15-C Phased Reduction Schedule

Introduced by **Decreto nº 10.997/2022**. Applies to Art. 15-B incisos IX, X, and XXII (the "cross-border payment arrangement track"). Incisos VII and XXII for credit card purchases were separately zeroed by Decreto 11.547/2023 ahead of this schedule.

The schedule is **self-executing** — each step takes effect on its date without any new decree. All future steps can be pre-seeded in a rate schedule table.

| Effective From | Effective Until | Rate | Decreto/Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2007-12-14 | 2023-01-01 | 6.38% | Original rate (Art. 15-B prior to 10.997) |
| **2023-01-02** | 2024-01-01 | **5.38%** | Step 1 — Decreto 10.997/2022; Decreto 11.374/2023 restored this after Bolsonaro-era premature zeroing |
| **2024-01-02** | 2025-01-01 | **4.38%** | Step 2 — self-executing |
| **2025-01-02** | 2026-01-01 | **3.38%** | Step 3 — **current (2025)** |
| **2026-01-02** | 2027-01-01 | **2.38%** | Step 4 — future, pre-legislated |
| **2027-01-02** | 2028-01-01 | **1.38%** | Step 5 — future, pre-legislated |
| **2028-01-02** | ∞ | **0%** | Final step — incisos IX, X, XX, XXI, XXII reach zero |
| **2029-01-02** | ∞ | **0%** | Full Art. 15-B zeroing (all remaining incisos) |

## The 2023 Legislative Saga — Detailed Timeline

### Background: Bolsonaro-era premature zeroing (late 2022)

In the final weeks of the Bolsonaro government, a series of decrees pre-emptively zeroed the Art. 15-C track rates ahead of schedule — specifically for international credit card purchases and crypto-related FX. Entering January 2023, these operations were temporarily at 0% instead of the 5.38% scheduled for 2023 under Art. 15-C.

### Decreto 11.374 — January 1, 2023 (Lula Day 1 reversal)

Lula's very first executive act was to revoke the Bolsonaro-era decrees, restoring the Art. 15-C schedule. As of January 2, 2023:
- International credit card purchases: back to **5.38%**
- ATM withdrawals abroad: back to **5.38%**
- Prepaid card loading: back to **5.38%**
- Outbound wire transfers: unchanged at **1.10%**

**Political impact:** Widely perceived as an "IOF hike" on Day 1, causing significant backlash from fintechs, digital banks, and the payments industry — even though it was technically a restoration to the already-legislated schedule.

**Crypto impact:** Crypto exchanges that had been temporarily at 0% for their FX contracts jumped back to 5.38%, creating a competitive distortion between Brazilian-domiciled exchanges (FX contract, 5.38%) and global platforms accessed from abroad (no FX contract, no IOF).

### Rate trajectory: May 2023 lobbying

Throughout mid-2023, the payments industry (Nubank, Wise, Remessa Online, ABECS) lobbied heavily to restore the zero rate specifically for credit cards, arguing that:
1. Foreign accounts (like Nomad or Wise global accounts) already had 0% because no Brazilian FX contract is formed
2. Brazilian credit cards were disadvantaged vs. global account products
3. The 5.38% distorted consumer behaviour toward less regulated alternatives

### Decreto 11.547 — October 25, 2023 (credit card zeroing, Lula partial U-turn)

The Lula government reversed course on credit cards specifically:
- **Zeroed** IOF on FX settlements from international credit card purchases (Art. 15-B VII and XXII)
- Effective: **October 26, 2023**
- Explicitly ahead of the Art. 15-C schedule (which would have reached 0% only in January 2028)

**What was NOT zeroed by Decreto 11.547:**
- Inciso IX (ATM withdrawals abroad) — continues on Art. 15-C track
- Inciso X (prepaid card loading / traveler checks) — continues on Art. 15-C track
- These remain at 3.38% in 2025

**Why the asymmetry?** The zero for credit cards was justified by market distortion (global accounts vs Brazilian cards). ATM withdrawals and prepaid cards were seen as more "optional" and less subject to the same competitive dynamics.

## Rate Determination Flowchart

```
FX operation
    │
    ├── Is it an inbound export revenue receipt?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B I, 0% (permanent)
    │
    ├── Is it a payment for imported GOODS (mercadorias)?
    │       → YES: Art. 16 I, 0% ISENÇÃO (not alíquota zero)
    │
    ├── Is it a foreign investor transacting in Brazilian markets?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B XVI or XVII, 0% (permanent)
    │
    ├── Is it a dividend / profit / interest on net equity remittance?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B XIII, 0% (permanent)
    │
    ├── Is it an international CREDIT CARD settlement?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B VII/XXII, 0% (since Decreto 11.547/2023)
    │
    ├── Is it an ATM cash withdrawal abroad?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B IX + Art. 15-C, 3.38% (2025)
    │
    ├── Is it loading an international PREPAID CARD or buying traveler checks?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B X + Art. 15-C, 3.38% (2025)
    │
    ├── Is it buying foreign BANKNOTES (espécie) for tourism?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B XX, 1.10%
    │
    ├── Is it an outbound WIRE TRANSFER by a resident?
    │   (personal, student support, medical, services, investment)
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B XXI, 1.10%
    │
    ├── Is it a SHORT-TERM FOREIGN LOAN (< 180-day avg. maturity)?
    │       → YES: Art. 15-B XII, 6.00%
    │
    └── None of the above
            → Art. 15-B (base/residual), 0.38%
```
